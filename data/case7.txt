Title: Sudhang Kumar Brahma v. BTC & Ors (Gauhati High Court, 2025)

[JUDICIAL DATA: 2025 PRECEDENT — BTC TENDER FAIRNESS]

Case Name: Sudhang Kumar Brahma v. The Bodoland Territorial Council and Anr Case No: WP(C)/1615/2025 Court: Gauhati High Court (Single Bench) Decision Date: May 14, 2025

1. The Dispute: A resident of Kokrajhar (Sudhang Kumar Brahma) challenged a Corrigendum (Notice) issued by the BTC Education Department. The BTC had suddenly changed the "rules of the game" mid-way through a tender for supplying school desks/benches.


2. Key Legal Ruling:

Arbitrary Changes: The BTC had raised the experience requirement and limited it only to those who had worked with the BTC before, disqualifying contractors who had worked with the State or Central Government.

The Court's Verdict: The Court held that BTC cannot change eligibility criteria without a rational justification. Restricting work only to "BTC-experienced" bidders was found to be "arbitrary and unreasonable."

Decision: The High Court struck down the BTC’s restrictive clause and ordered them to consider bidders based on their broader government work experience.
