Title: Chapter X of the Assam Land and Revenue Regulation, 1886 (Tribal Protections)

[KLLA DATASET: STATUTORY LAW — TRIBAL BELTS]

1. PROTECTIVE MEASURES (Sections 160–162)

Objective: To protect classes who, due to their "primitive condition" or "lack of material advantages," cannot safeguard their own land welfare.

Power to Notify: The State Government can notify specific areas as Tribal Belts and Blocks and specify "Protected Classes" (e.g., Plains Tribals, Hills Tribals, Tea tribes, Santhals, Koch-Rajbongshis).

2. RESTRICTIONS ON LAND DISPOSAL (Section 163)

Transfer Ban: Land in notified belts cannot be transferred, sold, or leased to persons who do not belong to the Protected Classes without the prior permission of the Deputy Commissioner.

Non-Protected Persons: Any occupation of land by a person not belonging to a protected class is considered "unauthorized" from the start.

3. EVICTION POWERS (Section 165)

Ejectment: The Deputy Commissioner has the power to eject any person who has no valid right to the land or who is not a member of a protected class.

Due Process: In periodically settled land (Patta land), the occupant must be served a one-month notice to remove buildings and crops before physical eviction.

4. BAR ON CIVIL COURTS (Section 167)

Jurisdiction: No Civil Court shall exercise jurisdiction in matters covered by this Chapter. All disputes must be handled by Revenue Officers or the BTC authorities.
