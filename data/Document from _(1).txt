Title: Bodoland Territorial Council (BTC) — Powers and Constitutional Status

[KLLA DATASET: CONSTITUTIONAL LAW — SIXTH SCHEDULE]

1. THE CONSTITUTIONAL SOURCE:

Authority: The BTC is an autonomous body created under the Sixth Schedule of the Constitution of India (Articles 244(2) and 275(1)).

Jurisdiction: It governs the Bodoland Territorial Region (BTR), comprising Kokrajhar, Chirang, Baksa, and Udalguri districts.

2. LEGISLATIVE & EXECUTIVE AUTONOMY:

Power to Regulate: The BTC has the power to make laws and policies regarding 40+ subjects, including Land, Forest (except reserved forests), Education, and Public Works.

Administrative Approval: For projects within its jurisdiction, the BTC is the primary authority for granting Administrative Approval (AA) and Technical Sanction (TS).

3. JUDICIAL REVIEW (Article 226):

Status: Despite its "Autonomous" status, the BTC is considered a "State" under Article 12.

High Court Power: All actions, tenders, and orders of the BTC are subject to the Writ Jurisdiction of the Gauhati High Court. If the BTC acts arbitrarily or changes rules unfairly, the High Court can strike down its decisions.

4. FINANCIAL RESPONSIBILITY:

The BTC is responsible for disbursing payments to contractors and individuals for works executed under its "Entrusted Departments."
