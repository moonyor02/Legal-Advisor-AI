SUBJECT: Assam Land and Revenue Regulation, 1886 - Chapter X (Tribal Belts & Blocks)

1. CORE JURISDICTION
- [span_0](start_span)Statutory Scope: Chapter X was specifically constituted to protect disadvantaged classes from land dispossession[span_0](end_span). 
- [span_1](start_span)[span_2](start_span)Protected Areas: Includes all designated Tribal Belts and Blocks within Assam, specifically covering areas under the Bodoland Territorial Council (BTC) such as Kokrajhar[span_1](end_span)[span_2](end_span).
- [span_3](start_span)[span_4](start_span)Eligible Persons: Land in these zones is strictly reserved for "eligible persons" belonging to protected classes as notified by the state[span_3](end_span)[span_4](end_span).

2. LAND TRANSFER & PROTECTION
- [span_5](start_span)[span_6](start_span)Restriction: No person can acquire rights over land in tribal belts except in compliance with the procedures of the 1886 Regulation[span_5](end_span)[span_6](end_span).
- [span_7](start_span)Transfer Audits: Authorities are legally required to ensure that all land transfer permissions in these blocks comply with statutory protections[span_7](end_span).

3. EVICTION PROCEDURES (SECTION 165)
- [span_8](start_span)[span_9](start_span)Unauthorized Occupation: Any person identified as "ineligible" or a "non-tribal" encroacher is liable for eviction[span_8](end_span)[span_9](end_span).
- [span_10](start_span)[span_11](start_span)Statutory Implementation: Eviction processes must be implemented strictly according to the 1886 Regulation and its allied rules[span_10](end_span)[span_11](end_span).

4. PROCEDURAL SAFEGUARDS
- [span_12](start_span)Rule-Based Action: All eviction proceedings must be rule-based and ensure full compliance with statutory requirements[span_12](end_span).
- [span_13](start_span)Official Duty: Deputy Commissioners are mandated to protect the integrity of tribal lands and enforce Chapter X provisions[span_13](end_span).
