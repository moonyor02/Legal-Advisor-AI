Title: The Limitation Act, 1963 (Statutory Provisions for Land)

1. THE SCHEDULE: LIMITATION PERIODS FOR IMMOVABLE PROPERTY

Article 64 (Suit based on Possession): > - Period: 12 Years.

Condition: For possession of immovable property based on previous possession and not on title.

Time Begins: From the date of dispossession.

Article 65 (Suit based on Title): > - Period: 12 Years.

Condition: For possession of immovable property or any interest therein based on title.

Time Begins: When the possession of the defendant becomes adverse (hostile) to the plaintiff.

2. SECTION 27: EXTINGUISHMENT OF RIGHT TO PROPERTY

Effect: At the determination of the period limited to any person for instituting a suit for possession of any property, his right to such property shall be extinguished.

Significance: This is a rare provision where the law doesn't just block the court case, it actually "kills" the legal ownership right if the owner is negligent for 12 years.

3. KEY DEFINITIONS (Section 2)

Prescribed Period: The period of limitation computed in accordance with the provisions of this Act.

Applicant: Includes a petitioner or any person through whom the applicant derives their right to apply.
