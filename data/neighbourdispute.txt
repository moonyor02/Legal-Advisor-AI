DOCUMENT TYPE: NEIGHBOR DISPUTE RESOLUTION
CONTEXT: BOUNDARY ENCROACHMENT AND RIGHT OF WAY

1. BOUNDARY DISPUTE (SIMANA NIRDHARAN):
If a neighbor occupies your land, apply for 'Demarcation' at the Circle Office. 
- Requirement: You must provide an up-to-date 'Khajna' (Land Revenue) receipt.
- Action: The Mandal will use the official village 'Trace Map' to fix boundary pillars.

2. RIGHT OF WAY (EASEMENT RIGHTS):
If a neighbor blocks a traditional path used for years, you can appeal to the 'Executive Magistrate' under Section 147 of the CrPC to have the path reopened.

3. ADVERSE POSSESSION PREVENTION:
Do not allow a neighbor to occupy your private land for more than 12 years. If they do, they may claim ownership through 'Adverse Possession.'
