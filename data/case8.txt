Case 1: Asom Basaok v. Ministry of Environment & Ors (PIL 2025)

Ruling (July 29, 2025): The Court ordered a "High-Level Meeting" of Chief Secretaries to build a roadmap for making all forests in Assam "Encroachment Free." It emphasized that biotic pressure (human settlement) must be removed to save biodiversity.

Case 2: Historic Eviction Order (August 18, 2025)

The Mandate: Chief Justice Ashutosh Kumar directed the State to fence forest borders with barbed wire and set up "functional check posts" to stop fresh encroachment.

Officer Liability: For the first time, the Court ruled that if fresh encroachment happens, the Forest Officer in charge will be held personally liable and face penal action.

Notice Rule: Encroachers must be given 15 days to reply to a notice and another 15 days to vacate before bulldozers are used.

Case 3: Ultapani & Ripu-Chirang Drive (April-June 2024)

Facts: Forest Dept cleared 33 families from Ultapani (Kokrajhar) and reclaimed 75 hectares in the Runikhata range.

Legal Outcome: The Court rejected claims of settlers from Karbi Anglong and Baksa who moved into Kokrajhar forests, stating that "alternative land" is not a prerequisite for evicting forest encroachers.
