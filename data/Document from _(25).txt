Title: The Assam Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1971

Metadata:

Statute: Assam Act XX of 1976 (Original 1971 Act)

Nodal Authority: District Commissioner (DC) / Circle Officer / BTC Revenue Dept

Key Context: Eviction from non-forest government land (Roads, PGR/VGR, Government Buildings)

2025 Update: Mandatory 15-day prior notice required per Gauhati High Court guidelines.

Key Statutory Provisions
Section 2(g) — Definition of "Unauthorised Occupation": The occupation by any person of public premises without authority, including the continuance in occupation after the authority (lease/grant) has expired or been cancelled.

Section 4 — Issue of Notice to Show Cause:

If the Estate Officer believes someone is in unauthorized occupation, they must issue a written notice.

Timeline: The notice must give the person at least 10 days (Statutory) / 15 days (Judicial 2025 update) to explain why they should not be evicted.

Service: Notice must be affixed to the outer door or a conspicuous part of the property.

Section 5 — Eviction Order:

After hearing the person's defense, if the officer is not satisfied, they issue a formal Order of Eviction.

Vacation Period: The occupant is usually given 30 days to vacate voluntarily after the order is published.

Use of Force: If they refuse to leave after 30 days, the officer may use "such force as may be necessary" to take possession.
